<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What to Do When Your RO Water Purifier Is Not Working</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
If your [RO water purifier is not working](https://www.rocareindia.com/ro-water-purifier-service-east-godawari), start with these safe basic checks before calling a professional:
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list {"ordered":true} -->
<ol class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Power Supply</strong>: Confirm the unit is switched on at the wall, the plug is firm in the socket, and the adaptor light is glowing. Try the same socket with another appliance to rule out a dead point or a tripped MCB .</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Inlet Tap</strong>: Make sure the inlet valve feeding the purifier is fully open. It can get knocked half-shut during cleaning, starving the unit of water .</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Filter Change Overdue</strong>: Think back to your last filter or membrane service. If it's months past due, a clogged filter is the likely cause of poor or no flow .</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Storage Tank Status</strong>: If you've just drawn several litres, the tank may simply be refilling. If the tank is full but no water dispenses, the issue is downstream of storage .</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ol>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>When to Call a Professional</strong>: If the basics check out but you still get no water or a thin, slow trickle, the problem is almost always a restriction in the system. A clogged pre-filter chokes flow to a trickle, fixed with a routine filter replacement during service . Any work involving opening the membrane housing, pump repairs, or electrical parts should be done by a trained technician .</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">What Shortens Membrane Life?</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Several factors explain why one homeowner sees a membrane fail after a year while another reaches four years or more :</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Feedwater Quality</strong>: Water quality is the single most important factor. High hardness minerals, TDS, iron, manganese, and chlorine cause more aggressive scaling and fouling. Hard-water conditions can cut membrane lifespan by about <strong>thirty percent</strong> compared with soft water .</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Chlorine Damage</strong>: TFC membranes are vulnerable to chlorine. If carbon pre-filters are exhausted or undersized, chlorine can reach the membrane and cause rapid chemical degradation, driving premature failure .</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Low Usage</strong>: Very low usage can lead to stagnant water, membrane fouling, and biofilm growth if the system is not flushed regularly .</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Operating Pressure</strong>: Typical household RO operating pressure is roughly 40-80 PSI. Too low pressure gives poor rejection and slow production. Too high pressure mechanically stresses the membrane .</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Monitoring Membrane Health</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The best way to determine when to replace your membrane is to monitor its performance using a TDS meter. Calculate the rejection rate:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:quote -->
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Rejection Rate = [(TDS in – TDS out) / TDS in] x 100</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></blockquote>
<!-- /wp:quote -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>A new membrane should have a rejection rate of 95-99%. Once the rejection rate <strong>consistently falls below 90%</strong>, it's time to replace the membrane . A declining rejection rate means more impurities are passing through, compromising water quality.</p>
